A Sweet Start to Homeownership

A smart negotiation that turned big issues into day‑one equity.

Read the full story Buyer testimonial
Kylie & Troy
Tampa Bay • Multiple‑offer market Listed: $599,900 Offer accepted: $605,000

Believe it or not, Tampa Bay is still seeing multiple‑offer situations. One strategy we’re seeing a lot right now? Pricing a home slightly under market value to spark competition and drive offers above list.

My buyers, Kylie & Troy, fell in love with a flipped home listed at $599,900. With limited information on the age or condition of major systems (as is common with flips), we went in with a smart, conservative offer at $605,000 — and won.

Then inspections revealed some major concerns.

We uncovered active termites & ant infestation in the attic, some minor electrical issues, a 20‑year‑old non‑functioning HVAC, a roof past its life expectancy, and an uninsurable water heater.

Most buyers would run.

Kylie & Troy said, “If we can get these things fixed, we’ll have essentially a brand‑new home.” So I did what I do best — I negotiated.

We submitted what might have been the longest repair request addendum I’ve ever written… the sellers agreed to roughly $35,000 in repairs if the buyers agreed to increase the purchase price by just $10,000 to offset part of the seller’s cost. Now, I’d call that a win/win — new HVAC, water heater, roof, and fully tented for termites for a relatively small increase in purchase price (don’t worry, we knew the home was worth more than $615,000).

And here’s where it became a win/win/WIN:

The home later appraised at $650,000.

Kylie & Troy walked into a beautifully updated home, brand‑new major systems, and about $35,000 in equity on Day One.

This is the kind of outcome I love creating for my clients. Every transaction has its hurdles, but I take the time to understand my buyers’ goals and how they’re thinking so we can make choices that support the outcome they truly want.

If you’re thinking about buying or selling in Tampa Bay, I’d love to help you strategize your own success story.
